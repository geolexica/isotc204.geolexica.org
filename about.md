# About the ISO/TC 204 ITS Vocabulary

## ISO/TC 204 — Intelligent Transport Systems

ISO/TC 204 is a technical committee of the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) responsible for the standardization of intelligent transport systems (ITS). The committee develops standards that support the integration of information and communication technologies with transport infrastructure and vehicles, enabling safer, more efficient, and more sustainable transportation systems.

Working closely with other ISO technical committees and international organizations, ISO/TC 204 addresses the full lifecycle of ITS — from system architecture and communications protocols to data management and performance evaluation.

## ISO 14812 — Intelligent Transport Systems — Vocabulary

At the core of this site is **ISO 14812**, the international standard that establishes the vocabulary for intelligent transport systems. The standard provides a comprehensive, authoritative set of definitions for terms used across all ITS domains, ensuring clear and unambiguous communication among stakeholders — from standards developers and system engineers to policymakers and researchers.

ISO 14812 defines concepts spanning:

- **ITS domains** — service domains, physical domains, and stakeholder perspectives
- **Transport entities** — travellers, vehicles, infrastructure, and the transport network
- **Data and communications** — messages, sessions, interfaces, and data concepts
- **System architecture** — functional, physical, and communications viewpoints
- **Performance and management** — levels of service, performance indicators, and monitoring concepts

### Why Terminology Matters

ITS is an inherently interdisciplinary field, drawing on expertise from automotive engineering, telecommunications, data science, urban planning, and public policy. Precise, standardized terminology is essential to:

- Ensure interoperability between ITS systems and components
- Provide a common language for international standards development
- Reduce ambiguity in specifications, regulations, and procurement documents
- Support the development of new ITS services and technologies
